Malawi - The Warm Heart of Africa

Malawi, known as "The Warm Heart of Africa," is a landlocked country in southeastern Africa. It's famous for its stunning Lake Malawi, which is the third largest lake in Africa and home to more species of fish than any other lake in the world.

The country is characterized by its friendly people, diverse cultures, and beautiful landscapes ranging from highlands to the Great Rift Valley. Malawi's economy is primarily agricultural, with tea, tobacco, and sugar being major exports.

Malawi gained independence from British colonial rule on July 6, 1964, and has since maintained a reputation as one of Africa's most peaceful nations.
